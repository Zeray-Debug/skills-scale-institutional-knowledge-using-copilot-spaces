## Summary of Project Management Processes

OctoAcme follows a customer-first, iterative delivery framework built on clear role ownership, data-informed decisions, and a strong culture of transparency and psychological safety.

### Key Lifecycle Stages

1. **Initiation** — Validate business needs, align stakeholders, and define high-level goals and timelines.
2. **Planning** — Break down deliverables, identify dependencies and risks, and prioritize the backlog.
3. **Execution & Tracking** — Run daily standups, weekly syncs, and track progress against milestones with formal reviews and demos.
4. **Release & Deployment** — Follow standardized processes for safe launches, post-release verification, and rollback playbooks.
5. **Retrospective & Continuous Improvement** — Capture learnings, drive process enhancements, and follow up on actionable items.

### Personas & Roles

| Role | Core Responsibility |
|------|---------------------|
| **Project Manager (PM)** | Coordinates delivery, schedules, risk, and communications |
| **Product Manager (PdM)** | Defines outcomes, prioritizes the backlog, and measures success |
| **Developers** | Implement features, collaborate on design and testability |
| **QA / Testing** | Validate quality and acceptance criteria |
| **Stakeholders** | Provide inputs and approvals |

### Communication Strategies

- **Daily standups** for the delivery team to surface blockers and align on priorities.
- **Weekly PM + PdM sync** to align on scope, risks, and roadmap.
- **Monthly stakeholder updates** to maintain executive and cross-team visibility.
- **Ad-hoc escalations** for risks or issues requiring immediate attention.

### Quality Assurance & Continuous Improvement

- QA validates features against acceptance criteria and the team's Definition of Done before release.
- Retrospectives are held at the end of each iteration to capture learnings and generate actionable improvements.
- A risk register is maintained throughout the project and reviewed regularly.
- Process changes are documented and communicated to the team to embed continuous improvement.
